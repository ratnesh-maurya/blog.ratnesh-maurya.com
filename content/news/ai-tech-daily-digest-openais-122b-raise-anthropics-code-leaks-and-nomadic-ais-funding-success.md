## Anthropic's Rough Month: Code Leaks and Security Scrutiny
![Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/GettyImages-1570465901.jpg?w=1024)
**Anthropic**, a company that has carefully cultivated a public image as the 'careful AI company' focused on AI risk research, experienced significant operational oversights this week. On Tuesday, the company accidentally included a file in version **2.1.88** of its **Claude Code** software package that exposed nearly **2,000 source code files** and over **512,000 lines of code**. This effectively revealed the complete architectural blueprint for one of its crucial products.

This incident marks the second time in a week that **Anthropic** has faced public scrutiny over internal data exposure. Days prior, Fortune reported that the company had inadvertently made almost **3,000 internal files** publicly accessible, including a draft blog post detailing an unannounced, powerful new AI model. A security researcher, Chaofan Shou, quickly identified and reported the Tuesday leak on X.

> This was a release packaging issue caused by human error, not a security breach.

**Anthropic** described the latest incident as a "release packaging issue caused by human error, not a security breach." While the company downplayed the severity publicly, the repeated incidents raise questions about internal processes and security protocols, especially for a company that prides itself on responsible AI development and is currently in a legal battle with the Department of Defense.

## OpenAI Closes Monster $122 Billion Funding Round at $852 Billion Valuation
![Sam Altman](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/GettyImages-2236544149.jpg?w=1024)
**OpenAI** has successfully concluded its largest funding round to date, raising an colossal **$122 billion** at an **$852 billion** valuation. This significant capital injection comes as the company gears up for a highly anticipated public market debut later this year. The funds will be strategically utilized to fuel **OpenAI's** substantial expenditures on cutting-edge **AI chips**, extensive data center infrastructure buildouts, and attracting top-tier talent in the competitive AI landscape.

The funding round saw strong participation from leading investors, with **SoftBank** co-leading alongside **Andreessen Horowitz**, D.E. Shaw Ventures, MGX, TPG, and T. Rowe Price Associates. Notable strategic partners **Amazon**, **Nvidia**, and **Microsoft** also contributed. Interestingly, approximately **$3 billion** of the total was sourced from individual retail investors through various bank channels, indicating a broader public interest in **OpenAI's** growth trajectory. Furthermore, **OpenAI** stock will soon be accessible to more individuals through its inclusion in several **ARK Invest** managed ETFs, further broadening its shareholder base prior to its reported IPO.

> OpenAI expanded its revolving credit facility to about **$4.7 billion**, underscoring its commitment to bolster financial flexibility for future compute and infrastructure investments.

In addition to the equity raise, **OpenAI** also expanded its revolving credit facility to approximately **$4.7 billion**, backed by several top global banks. The company confirmed that this facility remains undrawn, suggesting a proactive move to enhance its financial flexibility for future compute and infrastructure investments, rather than addressing immediate liquidity requirements. The press release detailing this raise was notably comprehensive, resembling a draft S-1 document, replete with flywheel metaphors and discussions on revenue per compute unit.

## Nomadic AI Raises $8.4 Million to Tackle Autonomous Vehicle Data Overload
![Mustafa Bal and Varun Krishnan, the co-founders of Nomadic AI.](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/founder_photo.jpeg?w=1024)
The proliferation of **autonomous vehicles** (AVs) and other robotics generates an astounding volume of video data, often amounting to thousands, if not millions, of hours. Organizing, cataloging, and extracting insights from this data is a monumental task that traditionally relies on human effort, a method that simply doesn't scale. Enter **Nomadic AI**, a startup founded by CEO **Mustafa Bal** and CTO **Varun Krishnan**, aiming to revolutionize how companies manage this critical information.

Many companies find that **95%** of their fleet data remains archived and underutilized. The challenge is amplified when searching for 'edge cases'—rare yet highly valuable events that are crucial for training and refining physical AI models. **Nomadic AI** addresses this by developing a platform that converts raw footage into a structured, searchable dataset using a suite of **vision language models**. This innovation allows for more effective fleet monitoring, the creation of unique datasets for **reinforcement learning**, and ultimately, faster iteration cycles for autonomous technology development.

> Nomadic AI’s platform turns raw footage into a structured, searchable dataset through vision language models, enabling better fleet monitoring and unique dataset creation for faster iteration.

**Nomadic AI** announced a successful **$8.4 million seed round** on Tuesday, achieving a post-money valuation of **$50 million**. The round was spearheaded by **TQ Ventures**, with contributions from **Pear VC** and **Jeff Dean**. This funding will enable **Nomadic AI** to expand its customer base and continue enhancing its platform. The company also recently secured first prize at **Nvidia GTC’s** pitch contest, further validating its innovative approach to autonomous data management.

## Re-evaluating Anthropic's 2023 AI Job Market Impact Study: Speculation vs. Reality
![How did Anthropic measure AI’s “theoretical capabilities” in the job market? - Ars Technica](https://cdn.arstechnica.net/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/GettyImages-2259634870-1152x648.jpg)
A graphic from a **2023 Anthropic report**, widely circulated this month, has sparked significant discussion about AI's potential impact on the job market. This graphic compares the "observed exposure" of occupations to Large Language Models (LLMs) with their "theoretical capability" across **22 job categories**. While current observed exposure is notable, the 'theoretical capability' section, suggesting LLM-based systems could perform at least **80 percent** of job tasks in diverse fields from "Arts & Media" to "Management," appears particularly alarming.

However, a closer examination of the report's methodology reveals a less chilling forecast. The "theoretical capability" figures are not based on **Anthropic's** own empirical testing of current LLM performance. Instead, they derive from a **2023** study by **Eloundou et al.** This study relied on **"heavily speculative educated guesses"** about how AI might improve human productivity, often assuming the development of "anticipated LLM-powered software" rather than direct replacement of human roles.

> The "theoretical capability" numbers in Anthropic's 2023 job market report are based on speculative assumptions about future LLM-powered software, not empirical testing of current AI capabilities.

This means the broad blue field on the graph, implying vast job displacement, actually represents forward-looking projections for future software built atop LLMs, rather than a realistic assessment of current AI capabilities. The study's rubric for assessing impact included categories that assume significant future "additional software" development, which broadly influences the perceived potential of LLMs to impact jobs. Thus, the report paints a picture of potential productivity enhancement rather than an immediate or widespread takeover of human jobs.
