## OpenAI Alums Launch New Venture Fund, Zero Shot, Aiming for $100 Million

![Zero Shot fund founders](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/zsf-photo-02.jpg?w=680)

A new venture capital fund named **Zero Shot** has successfully completed its first close, moving towards its ambitious **$100 million** funding goal. This fund is noteworthy for its strong connections to **OpenAI**, with several founding partners being former key figures from the AI research and deployment company. The fund has already begun making initial investments, signaling its active presence in the startup ecosystem.

The co-founding team includes **Evan Morikawa**, who was the former head of applied engineering at **OpenAI** during the launches of **DALL·E**, **ChatGPT**, and **Codex**. Also among the founders is **Andrew Mayne**, **OpenAI's** original prompt engineer and host of _The OpenAI podcast_, alongside **Shawn Jain**, an engineer and former researcher at **OpenAI** who also founded his own GenAI startup, **Synthefy**. These individuals are leveraging their deep expertise in AI to identify and support the next generation of innovative startups.

> **Zero Shot**, a new venture fund with deep ties to **OpenAI**, has made its first close on its **$100 million** goal and has already begun investing, marking a significant development for the AI startup landscape.

![“The problem is Sam Altman”: OpenAI Insiders don’t trust CEO - Ars Technica](https://cdn.arstechnica.net/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/GettyImages-2236544323-640x424.jpg)

## OpenAI's Vision for the AI Economy: Public Wealth Funds, Robot Taxes, and a Four-Day Work Week

![Sam Altman, CEO of OpenAI, sitting in front of American flag](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/GettyImages-2226496284.jpg?w=1024)

**OpenAI** has put forward a series of bold policy proposals designed to navigate the economic implications of superintelligent machines. These recommendations aim to reshape wealth distribution and work structures in what **OpenAI** terms the "intelligence age." The proposals merge concepts typically associated with left-leaning economic policies, such as public wealth funds and expanded social safety nets, with a foundational capitalist, market-driven framework.

These proposals come at a time of escalating public anxiety surrounding AI, fueled by concerns over potential job displacement, the concentration of wealth, and the rapid buildout of data centers. **OpenAI's** vision includes mechanisms like a "robot tax" and the widespread adoption of a four-day work week, suggesting a radical re-imagining of how society can manage and benefit from advanced AI.

> **OpenAI** proposes a blend of public wealth funds, robot taxes, and a four-day work week to manage the economic changes brought by an "intelligence age."

## Claude, OpenClaw, and the New Reality: AI Agents Unleash Chaos and Potential

![Chaos](https://venturebeat.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fimages.ctfassets.net%2Fjdtwqhzvc2n1%2F33OB5cKXtts9VZ7sMyzGew%2F7454f3b529fbde6e78746d28b720e4c4%2FChaos.png%3Fw%3D1000%26q%3D100&w=3840&q=85)

The era of agentic AI has definitively arrived, bringing with it both unprecedented capabilities and new challenges. What began as simple interactions with chatbots like **ChatGPT** in **2022** has now evolved into a broader discussion about job security and the autonomous capabilities of machines. Powerful new autonomous agents such as **Claude Cowork** and **OpenClaw** are at the forefront of this shift, pushing the boundaries of what AI can accomplish.

**OpenClaw**, formerly known as **Moltbot** and **Clawdbot**, has quickly garnered over **150,000 GitHub stars** and is being deployed locally with deep system access, performing tasks from inbox triaging to content curation. **Google's Antigravity** acts as a coding agent that accelerates development from prompt to production. Meanwhile, **Anthropic's Claude Cowork** has already caused significant market shifts, particularly in legal-tech and SaaS stocks, by automating tasks like contract review and NDA triage. These agents promise enhanced productivity but also highlight increased risks of misuse and the critical need for trust in providers.

> The rapid rise of autonomous AI agents like **OpenClaw** and **Claude Cowork** signals a transformative, albeit chaotic, shift in technology, raising questions about trust and control as they gain system access and domain expertise.

## Cursor's $2 Billion Bet: The IDE Is Now a Fallback, Not the Default

![Featued image for: Cursor’s $2 billion bet: The IDE is now a fallback, not the default](https://cdn.thenewstack.io/media/2026/04/a0f52b0f-puzzle-creative-x0nz565wjq-unsplash-1024x603.jpg)

The landscape of software development tools is undergoing a profound transformation, spearheaded by the emergence of sophisticated AI agents. **Cursor's** recent **$2 billion** valuation underscores a significant industry shift: the traditional Integrated Development Environment (**IDE**) is increasingly becoming a secondary tool, rather than the primary interface for developers. This change is driven by AI agents that can generate, debug, and even deploy code, pushing the **IDE** into a fallback role.

This trend suggests that developers will interact with AI agents to perform complex coding tasks, leveraging the **IDE** primarily for validation, fine-tuning, or when direct human intervention is preferred for specific challenges. This redefines the workflow, making AI agents the default starting point for many development processes and signaling a new era where AI deeply integrates into every step of the software creation lifecycle.

> **Cursor's** **$2 billion** valuation highlights a major industry shift where AI agents are making the **IDE** a fallback option, signaling a future where AI becomes the default starting point for development tasks.
