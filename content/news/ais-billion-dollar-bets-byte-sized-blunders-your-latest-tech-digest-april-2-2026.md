## Anthropic's Rocky Week: Code Leaks and Internal Files Exposed

![Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/GettyImages-1570465901.jpg?w=1024)

**Anthropic**, a company that has carefully cultivated a public image as the 'careful AI company' deeply committed to AI risk research, has recently faced a series of operational blunders. This past week saw two significant incidents that contradicted its ethos. Days prior to the latest incident, it was reported that **Anthropic** had accidentally made nearly **3,000** internal files publicly available. These files even included a draft blog post detailing a powerful new model that had not yet been officially announced.

The more recent incident occurred on Tuesday, March 31, 2026, when **Anthropic** released version **2.1.88** of its **Claude Code** software package. This release inadvertently included a file that exposed nearly **2,000** source code files and over **512,000** lines of code, effectively providing the complete architectural blueprint for one of its key products. A security researcher, Chaofan Shou, quickly noticed the exposure and publicized it on X. **Anthropic** described the event as a "release packaging issue caused by human error, not a security breach," suggesting internal issues rather than external attacks.

> While **Anthropic** positions itself as the careful AI company, recent incidents highlight significant internal control issues that undermine this public identity.

## OpenAI Secures Staggering $122 Billion in Funding, Eyes IPO

![Sam Altman](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/GettyImages-2236544149.jpg?w=1024)

**OpenAI** has successfully closed its largest funding round to date, securing a massive **$122 billion** in committed capital at an impressive **$852 billion** valuation. This significant capital infusion is earmarked to bolster **OpenAI**'s resources for crucial investments in AI chips, data center infrastructure expansion, and attracting top-tier talent. The funding round saw diverse participation, co-led by **SoftBank**, **Andreessen Horowitz**, **D.E. Shaw Ventures**, **MGX**, **TPG**, and **T. Rowe Price Associates**, with additional contributions from strategic partners like **Amazon**, **Nvidia**, and **Microsoft**.

Notably, approximately **$3 billion** of the total raise came from individual investors channeled through bank platforms, indicating a broader public interest in the private company. **OpenAI** is also set to be included in several ETFs managed by **ARK Invest**, further democratizing access to its stock in anticipation of its reportedly upcoming IPO this year. The company also expanded its revolving credit facility to about **$4.7 billion**, underscoring its commitment to financial flexibility and accelerating its compute and infrastructure spending without immediate liquidity needs.

> **OpenAI**'s record-breaking funding round, including significant retail investor participation, positions it with an immense war chest as it prepares for an anticipated public market debut.

## Nomadic AI Secures $8.4 Million to Streamline Autonomous Vehicle Data

![Mustafa Bal and Varun Krishnan, the co-founders of Nomadic AI.](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/founder_photo.jpeg?w=1024)

**Nomadic AI**, a startup co-founded by CEO Mustafa Bal and CTO Varun Krishnan, has successfully raised **$8.4 million** in a seed funding round, valuing the company at **$50 million** post-money. This investment aims to tackle the significant challenge of managing the enormous volume of video data generated by autonomous machines like self-driving cars, robots, and autonomous construction equipment. Currently, the arduous task of organizing and cataloging this data, particularly for identifying crucial edge cases that rarely occur, primarily relies on human review, a process that is inefficient and doesn't scale.

**Nomadic AI**'s innovative platform addresses this by transforming raw footage into a structured, searchable dataset using a collection of vision language models. This technology enables more effective fleet monitoring and facilitates the creation of unique datasets essential for reinforcement learning and faster iterative development of AI models. The seed round was led by **TQ Ventures**, with participation from **Pear VC** and **Jeff Dean**. The funding will enable **Nomadic** to expand its customer base and further refine its platform. The company also recently won first prize at **Nvidia GTC**'s pitch contest, highlighting the industry's recognition of its solution.

> **Nomadic AI**'s **$8.4 million** seed round empowers its mission to automate the critical, yet labor-intensive, process of organizing and extracting value from the vast datasets generated by autonomous vehicles, accelerating AI model development.

## Deconstructing Anthropic's AI Job Market Impact Report: Theoretical vs. Reality

![How did Anthropic measure AI’s “theoretical capabilities” in the job market? - Ars Technica](https://cdn.arstechnica.net/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/GettyImages-2259634870-1152x648.jpg)

A graphic depicting AI's theoretical capability to perform a vast majority of job tasks has been widely circulated, stemming from a **2023 Anthropic report** on the labor market impacts of AI. This graphic compares the current "observed exposure" of occupations to LLMs (in red) with the "theoretical capability" of those same LLMs (in blue) across **22** job categories. At first glance, the "theoretical capability" field suggests that LLM-based systems could eventually perform at least **80 percent** of individual "job tasks" across a wide range of human occupations, including "Arts & Media," "Office & Admin," "Legal, Business & Finance," and even "Management."

However, a closer examination of the basis for these "theoretical capability" numbers reveals a less alarming picture. The **LLM** "theoretical capability" baseline cited by **Anthropic** is not based on empirical testing of its own models. Instead, it relies on outdated and highly speculative educated guesses about where AI is likely to enhance human productivity, rather than where it will completely replace human jobs. These projections incorporate forward-looking assumptions about "anticipated LLM-powered software," which may not materialize as predicted or may serve to augment rather than supplant human roles entirely.

> The widely cited **Anthropic** report on AI's "theoretical capabilities" in the job market is based on speculative, 2023-era assumptions about future **LLM-powered software**, not current empirical evidence of AI's ability to take over jobs.
