## Google introduces a faster, cheaper image generator with Nano Banana 2 Lite
![Google image models](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/06/google-image-models.png?w=1024)
**Google** has unveiled **Nano Banana 2 Lite**, the latest iteration of its in-house AI video and image generator. This new version is touted by the company as being substantially faster and more economical than its predecessor.

The model boasts significantly reduced latency, capable of producing images in just **four seconds**. This speed positions it as an ideal tool for users requiring rapid iteration and high-volume image production, such as during workshops or content drafting. Furthermore, the cost-effectiveness is notable, priced at just **$0.034 per 1,000 images**, making it an affordable solution for creators looking to scale their content efforts.

> **Google's Nano Banana 2 Lite** democratizes high-volume AI image generation with its speed and affordability, enabling rapid content creation and iteration.

## Acti puts AI agents directly into your smartphone keyboard
![Acti puts AI agents directly into your smartphone keyboard - TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/06/Landscape_Promo.png?w=1024)
**Acti**, a Singapore-based startup, has launched an innovative agentic keyboard for both **iOS** and **Android** devices. This new keyboard goes beyond typical word suggestions, integrating AI agents directly into the typing experience to perform actions on behalf of the user.

The core idea behind **Acti** is to eliminate the constant app-switching users endure when seeking AI assistance. According to **Acti** founder and CEO Young Wang, this keyboard brings AI tools directly into frequently used applications such as email, messaging, and social media. This allows users to leverage AI capabilities without leaving their current context, streamlining workflows and enhancing productivity.

> **Acti** is transforming mobile interaction by embedding AI agents directly into the smartphone keyboard, allowing users to perform AI-powered actions without ever leaving their current app.

[🔗 Read more](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/30/acti-puts-ai-agents-directly-into-your-smartphone-keyboard/)

## There's Now An OpenClaw App For iOS And Android Phones
![Illustration showing five smartphone screens displaying the OpenClaw app in use](https://www.engadget.com/img/gallery/theres-now-an-openclaw-app-for-ios-and-android-phones/intro-1782770668.jpg)
**OpenClaw**, an open-source project, has officially released standalone applications for **iOS** and **Android** smartphones. This move marks the arrival of agentic AI directly onto the App Store and Play Store, offering users a new way to interact with AI assistants on their mobile devices.

Users of the new **OpenClaw** apps can now chat with the AI assistant and grant it access to various device components, including the camera, screen, location, photos, contacts, calendar, and reminders. The project gained significant traction after its founder, Peter Steinberger, joined **OpenAI** earlier this year, with **OpenAI** providing unspecified support to the **OpenClaw Foundation**, which now publishes the apps. This development is particularly noteworthy given **Apple's** historically stringent review process for agentic AI tools due to security concerns.

> The launch of **OpenClaw**'s **iOS** and **Android** apps signifies a major step in bringing powerful, agentic AI directly to smartphone users, despite prior regulatory hurdles.

## OpenAI is teasing new hardware… for Codex
![openai-codex-hardware](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/06/openai-codex-hardware.png?quality=90&strip=all&crop=2.4752475247525%2C0%2C95.049504950495%2C100&w=2400)
**OpenAI** has hinted at new hardware development, specifically a device tailored for its AI-powered coding tool, **Codex**. A video posted to X on Monday showcased a square-shaped device featuring several buttons, accompanied by the caption: “Your favorite **Codex** shortcuts are getting an upgrade.” The launch is slated for **July 15th**.

This new device is a collaboration with **Work Louder**, a company known for its mechanical keyboards and macro pads that allow for mappable keys, dials, and switches. While it's not the much-anticipated AI device **OpenAI** is reportedly developing with former **Apple** designer Jony Ive, the silhouette of the new hardware bears a resemblance to **Work Louder's Creator Micro 2** macro pad. This suggests the device will offer customizable shortcuts and actions, similar to how **Figma** previously partnered with **Work Louder** for a macro pad with preconfigured shortcuts.

> **OpenAI**'s upcoming hardware collaboration with **Work Louder** will enhance developer productivity by providing a dedicated physical interface for **Codex** shortcuts and actions.

## Mapping Europe’s AI Workforce Opportunity
![Flowchart showing how EU occupations map to AI jobs transition archetypes, including occupations with higher automation potential, occupations likely to reorganize, occupations that may grow with AI, and occupations with less immediate change.](https://images.ctfassets.net/kftzwdyauwt9/gaXhta3JZscfpc1wbIdjY/17d982d034064e3197041a6d00f7b89e/occupations-desktop-light.svg?w=3840&q=90)
**OpenAI Economic Research** has released a new report, "The AI Jobs Transition Framework for the EU," which extends its previous framework for the United States to the European labor market. The report addresses critical questions about AI's labor market impact, its geographic and temporal effects, and how to ensure a beneficial transition for all.

Utilizing the official European Skills, Competences, Qualifications and Occupations (**ESCO**) taxonomy and **Eurostat** employment data, the study examines how AI capabilities might lead to near-term occupational changes across **EU** member states. A key finding is that, when compared to the **U.S.**, the **EU** has a smaller proportion of employment in occupations with higher near-term automation potential. The framework categorizes occupations into four archetypes: those that may grow with AI, those with higher automation potential, those likely to reorganize, and those facing less immediate change. These categories serve as a planning map to anticipate where adjustment pressures and opportunities may arise.

> **OpenAI's** new report provides a crucial "planning map" for the **EU**, detailing how AI will reshape the workforce by identifying specific job archetypes and regions susceptible to varied impacts, highlighting that jobs don't transition as easily as AI capabilities cross borders.
