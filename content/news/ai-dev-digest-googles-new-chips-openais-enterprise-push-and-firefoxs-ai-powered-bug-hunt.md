## Google Cloud Launches Two New AI Chips to Compete with Nvidia
![Google chip](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Google-TPU8.jpg?w=1024)
**Google Cloud** has unveiled its eighth generation of custom-built **AI chips**, known as **Tensor Processing Units (TPUs)**, strategically divided into two specialized offerings. The **TPU 8t** is engineered for **AI model training**, while the **TPU 8i** is specifically designed for **inference**, which encompasses the ongoing usage of **AI models** post-deployment.

The company asserts that these new **TPUs** deliver substantial performance enhancements over their predecessors. Key improvements include up to **3x faster AI model training**, an **80% better performance-per-dollar ratio**, and the capability to link over **1 million TPUs** into a unified cluster. This development aims to provide customers with significantly more compute power at reduced energy consumption and cost.

> **Google Cloud's** dual **TPU 8t** and **TPU 8i** chips offer up to **3x faster AI model training** and **80% better performance per dollar**, signaling a direct challenge to competitors like **Nvidia** in the specialized **AI hardware** market.

## OpenAI Teams Up with Infosys to Bring AI Tools to More Businesses
![Binary code and OpenAI logo](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/12/GettyImages-2021258442.jpg?w=1024)
**OpenAI** has forged a partnership with **Infosys**, an Indian IT services giant, to integrate its artificial intelligence tools, including the notable coding assistant **Codex**, into **Infosys's Topaz AI platform**. This collaboration aims to assist **Infosys's** clientele in modernizing software development, automating workflows, and deploying **AI systems** at scale.

The initial focus of this integration will be on critical areas such as **software engineering**, **legacy modernization**, and **DevOps**. This strategic move by **OpenAI** to team up with global IT service providers like **Infosys** underscores a broader industry trend of scaling **AI adoption** across various businesses, especially as IT firms face pressures from slowing client spending and the rapid advancements in generative AI.

> The partnership between **OpenAI** and **Infosys** will embed **OpenAI's AI tools**, including **Codex**, into the **Topaz AI platform**, enabling **Infosys's** clients to accelerate software development and **AI deployment** at scale.

## OpenAI’s Updated Image Generator Can Now Pull Information from the Web
![An image generated by ChatGPT Images 2.0.](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/04/chatgpt-images-interior.png?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0%2C0%2C100%2C100&w=2400)
**OpenAI** has rolled out a significant update to its **AI-powered image generator**, now branded as **ChatGPT Images 2.0**, which introduces new "thinking capabilities." This enhancement allows the generator to search the web for information, enabling it to create multiple, more sophisticated images from a single prompt. The update is powered by **OpenAI's** new **GPT Image 2 model**.

Subscribers to **ChatGPT Plus, Pro, Business**, and **Enterprise** can access these advanced capabilities, which include creating visual explainers from uploaded files and reasoning through image structure before generation. **ChatGPT Images 2.0** can now produce up to **eight images simultaneously** while maintaining consistent characters, objects, and styles, making it ideal for tasks like generating manga pages or social graphics. Furthermore, it supports resolutions up to **2K** and various aspect ratios, with significant improvements in generating text in non-Latin languages like Japanese, Korean, Chinese, Hindi, and Bengali.

> **ChatGPT Images 2.0** now features "thinking capabilities" powered by **GPT Image 2**, allowing it to leverage web information to generate up to **eight consistent and sophisticated images** at once, improving text generation and offering **2K resolution**.

## Mozilla Used Anthropic’s Mythos to Find and Fix 271 Bugs in Firefox
![Mozilla Used Anthropic’s Mythos to Find and Fix 271 Bugs in Firefox - WIRED](https://media.wired.com/photos/69e6df06b405035df7b7ce2e/191:100/w_1280,c_limit/security_GettyImages-2214075816.jpg)
**Mozilla** has announced that its latest **Firefox 150** browser release incorporates protections for **271 vulnerabilities** that were identified using early access to **Anthropic's Mythos Preview**. This initiative highlights the profound impact that new **AI models** can have on cybersecurity and vulnerability hunting.

According to **Bobby Holley**, **Firefox's** Chief Technology Officer, these **AI tools** have dramatically changed the landscape by providing automated techniques capable of covering the full spectrum of vulnerability-inducing bugs. While acknowledging the significant resources and discipline required to manage the influx of bugs uncovered by **AI**, **Mozilla** deems this effort essential for user security, especially given that these powerful capabilities will inevitably be accessible to attackers.

> **Mozilla** leveraged **Anthropic's Mythos Preview** to uncover and fix **271 vulnerabilities** in **Firefox**, demonstrating the critical role **AI** can play in rapidly enhancing software security against evolving threats.
