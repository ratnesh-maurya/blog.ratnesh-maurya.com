## Microsoft is working on yet another OpenClaw-like agent
![Microsoft Windows with Claw logo](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Windows-Claw.png?w=1024)
**Microsoft** is reportedly developing and testing new features to integrate **OpenClaw-like** capabilities into its existing **Microsoft 365 Copilot** tool. This move aims to provide enterprise customers with advanced agentic functionalities, while addressing security concerns associated with the open-source **OpenClaw** agent, which has been noted for its risks.

The company confirmed to *The Information* that these features would be specifically designed for enterprise users, offering enhanced security controls. This effort aligns with **Microsoft's** broader strategy of introducing multiple agentic tools in recent months, including the **Copilot Cowork** announced in March. The goal is to create agents that run locally on a user's computer, performing tasks on their behalf.

> **Microsoft's** focus on a more secure, locally-running agent for **Microsoft 365 Copilot** signals a strategic move to offer robust, enterprise-grade autonomous AI capabilities.

## OpenAI executive sends internal memo: ‘The market is as competitive as I have ever seen it’
![OpenAI released a report breaking down how people use ChatGPT and who they are.](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2025/04/STK_414_AI_CHATBOT_R2_CVirginia_B.jpg?quality=90&strip=all&crop=16.666666666667%2C0%2C66.666666666667%2C100&w=2400)
**OpenAI's** Chief Revenue Officer, **Denise Dresser**, recently sent a four-page internal memo to employees, obtained by *The Verge*, highlighting the fierce competition within the **AI** market. Dresser stated, "The market is as competitive as I have ever seen it," emphasizing the critical need for **OpenAI** to 'build a moat' around its **AI** products to retain users in an environment where switching between models is easy.

The memo also detailed **OpenAI's** strategic direction, stressing the importance of growing its enterprise business and transitioning into a "platform company with multiple entry points and one integrated enterprise offering." Dresser criticized rival **Anthropic**, suggesting its "coding focus gave them an early wedge" but arguing against being a "single-product company in a platform war." She also accused **Anthropic** of inflating its run rate and making a "strategic misstep" by not acquiring sufficient compute resources. Both companies are reportedly planning to go public this year.

> **OpenAI** is shifting its strategy to focus on multi-product adoption and an integrated enterprise offering, viewing the **AI** market as intensely competitive, particularly against **Anthropic**.

## When AI stops being an experiment and becomes a new development model
![When AI stops being an experiment and becomes a new development model - TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/2604_AI-in-SDLC_Vention%E2%80%93TechCrunch.png?w=1024)
**Artificial intelligence** is rapidly transforming from an experimental tool into a foundational development model for software leaders. While many developers are testing **AI copilots** and integrating **AI** into personal workflows, a significant challenge remains: translating individual productivity gains into tangible improvements in delivery for engineering organizations.

According to an article presented by **TC Brand Studio**, the primary hurdle isn't the technology itself but the way work is structured. Most teams attempt to layer **AI** onto existing workflows not designed for it, which can lead to fragmentation, increased cognitive load, and reliability risks, ultimately resulting in more rework rather than reduced effort. The key to successful **AI** integration lies in restructuring workflows to align with **AI's** capabilities, ensuring that **AI** truly enhances delivery in a way that benefits the business.

> For **AI** to move beyond experimentation and genuinely improve software development, organizations must restructure their workflows rather than simply layering **AI** tools onto existing processes.

## At the HumanX conference, everyone was talking about Claude
![Claude ad on side of building](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/12/Claude-ad-e1733259907871.jpg?w=1024)
At the **HumanX AI conference** in San Francisco, **Anthropic's Claude** emerged as the dominant topic of conversation among thousands of tech professionals. Discussions at the **Moscone Center** centered on agentic **AI** and its impact on business, with **Claude** frequently highlighted as the most popular chatbot for automating business and coding tasks.

Attendees and vendors consistently mentioned **Claude**, with many expressing a preference for it over **OpenAI's ChatGPT**. One vendor specifically noted that his team extensively uses **Claude**, perceiving that **ChatGPT** and **OpenAI** have declined in recent performance. This sentiment, reportedly, is not unique, indicating a growing trend in the **AI** community's perception of these competing chatbots.

> **Anthropic's Claude** is rapidly gaining mindshare and preference over **ChatGPT** in agentic **AI** applications, reflecting a significant shift in developer and enterprise sentiment.
