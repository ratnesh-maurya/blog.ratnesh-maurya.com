## OpenAI, not yet public, raises $3B from retail investors in monster $122B fund raise
![Sam Altman](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/GettyImages-2236544149.jpg?w=1024)
**OpenAI**, still a private entity, has successfully completed its largest funding round to date, amassing a monumental **$122 billion**. This capital infusion pushes the company's valuation to an astonishing **$852 billion**, signaling strong investor confidence ahead of its anticipated public market debut this year. The funds are earmarked for significant investments in **AI chips**, data center infrastructure, and attracting top-tier talent.

Notably, approximately **$3 billion** of this funding originated from individual investors channeled through bank platforms. Key investors in this round include **SoftBank**, **Andreessen Horowitz**, D.E. Shaw Ventures, MGX, TPG, and T. Rowe Price Associates, with additional participation from industry giants like **Amazon**, **Nvidia**, and **Microsoft**. The company also expanded its revolving credit facility to roughly **$4.7 billion**, underscoring its proactive approach to financial flexibility as it scales its operations.

> The inclusion of **OpenAI** in several **ARK Invest ETFs** is poised to democratize access to its private stock, broadening its shareholder base as it lays the groundwork for a potential IPO.

## OpenAI raises $122 billion to accelerate the next phase of AI
![OAI Astral 1x1 Art Card](https://images.ctfassets.net/kftzwdyauwt9/7k855PuyjQwz6v2uBnntj1/1fd85401e9f0183d5352b1696534409e/OAI_Astral_1x1_Art_Card__1_.png?w=3840&q=90&fm=webp)
**OpenAI** officially announced the close of its latest funding round, securing **$122 billion** in committed capital, which firmly places its post-money valuation at **$852 billion**. This substantial investment is intended to "accelerate the next phase of AI," as the company continues to establish itself as a core infrastructure provider for AI development globally.

The company emphasized its rapid growth, highlighting that **ChatGPT** was the fastest technology platform to reach **10 million** and then **100 million** users, with aspirations to reach **1 billion** weekly active users soon. Furthermore, **OpenAI** achieved **$1 billion** in revenue within a year of **ChatGPT**'s launch, escalating to **$1 billion** per quarter by the end of **2024**, and currently generating **$2 billion** in revenue per month. This growth rate is reportedly four times faster than early internet and mobile era defining companies like **Alphabet** and **Meta**.

> This funding not only signifies robust commercial scale but also underscores a mission to widen the benefits of AI by putting useful intelligence into people's hands early and broadly.

## Nomadic raises $8.4 million to wrangle the data pouring off autonomous vehicles
![Mustafa Bal and Varun Krishnan, the co-founders of Nomadic AI.](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/founder_photo.jpeg?w=1024)
Developing advanced autonomous systems, whether for self-driving cars or robotic equipment, generates an overwhelming volume of video data—often thousands to millions of hours. Managing, organizing, and effectively utilizing this data presents a significant challenge, especially when identifying crucial 'edge cases' that are vital for refining AI models but occur infrequently. Addressing this, **Nomadic AI**, a startup founded by CEO **Mustafa Bal** and CTO **Varun Krishnan**, aims to transform raw footage into structured, searchable datasets.

**Nomadic AI** announced an **$8.4 million** seed round, bringing its post-money valuation to **$50 million**. This funding, led by **TQ Ventures** with participation from **Pear VC** and **Jeff Dean**, will enable the company to expand its customer base and enhance its platform. Their solution leverages a collection of vision language models to facilitate better fleet monitoring and the creation of unique datasets essential for reinforcement learning and faster iteration of AI models.

> **Nomadic AI** is tackling the critical bottleneck of data organization for autonomous systems, ensuring that valuable fleet data, often archived and unused, can be transformed into actionable insights.

## ScaleOps raises $130M to improve computing efficiency amid AI demand
![ScaleOps raises $130M to improve computing efficiency amid AI demand - TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/ScaleOps.jpg?w=680)
As the demand for AI accelerates, companies are grappling with the issue of wasted computing resources, including idle **GPUs** and over-provisioned workloads, leading to escalating cloud costs. **ScaleOps**, a startup co-founded by **Yodar Shafrir**, aims to tackle this problem by developing software that automates the real-time management and reallocation of computing resources, claiming to reduce cloud and AI infrastructure costs by as much as **80%**.

**ScaleOps** recently secured a **$130 million** Series C funding round, valuing the company at **$800 million**. The round was led by **Insight Partners**, with continued investment from existing backers such as **Lightspeed Venture Partners**, **NFX**, **Glilot Capital Partners**, and **Picture Capital**. Shafrir, who previously worked at **Run:ai** (acquired by **Nvidia**), observed firsthand the difficulties companies faced in managing complex AI workloads, particularly with tools like **Kubernetes** often relying on static configurations that struggle with dynamic demand.

> **ScaleOps** addresses the critical issue of compute mismanagement, offering a solution to significantly cut down on the vast amounts of expensive resources currently being wasted in the booming AI sector.

## Qodo raises $70M for code verification as AI coding scales
![Abstract technology image of running program code on digital data wave.](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/GettyImages-2080252145.jpg?w=1024)
With AI coding tools generating billions of lines of code monthly, a new challenge has emerged: verifying that the software functions as intended. **Qodo**, a New York-headquartered startup, is positioning itself at the forefront of this next phase of software development by building AI agents for comprehensive code review, testing, and governance.

**Qodo** has successfully raised a **$70 million** Series B round, bringing its total funding to **$120 million**. The round was led by **Qumra Capital**, with participation from **Maor Ventures**, **Phoenix Venture Partners**, **S Ventures**, **Square Peg**, **Susa Ventures**, **TLV Partners**, **Vine Ventures**, **Peter Welender** (**OpenAI**), and **Clara Shih** (**Meta**). The company aims to provide a crucial layer of trust for AI-generated code, as enterprises rapidly adopt tools like **OpenClaw** and **Claude Code** but often find that increased output doesn't always equate to reliable or secure software.

> **Qodo** is focused on transforming how code changes impact entire systems, moving beyond simple change detection to factor in organizational standards, historical context, and risk tolerance, thereby enabling companies to manage AI-generated code with greater confidence.

[🔗 Read more](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/30/qodo-bets-on-code-verification-as-ai-coding-scales-raises-70m/)
