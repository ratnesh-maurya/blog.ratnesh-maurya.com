## OpenAI Scraps Sora Amidst Profit Pressure and Fierce Competition
![Why OpenAI killed Sora - The Verge](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2025/09/STK201_SAM_ALTMAN_CVIRGINIA_B.jpg?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0%2C10.732984293194%2C100%2C78.534031413613&w=1200)

In a surprising move, **OpenAI** has announced the discontinuation of its video-generation app, **Sora**, and will no longer integrate video generation into **ChatGPT**. This decision, made public on Tuesday, also involves winding down a **$1 billion Disney deal** and a shuffle in executive roles. The company is now aggressively focused on profitability, aiming to lose less money and raise an additional **$10 billion** in funding, pushing its latest round to over **$120 billion** total.

Industry sources suggest that **Sora** consumed a massive amount of compute resources without yielding proportional financial returns, and it was reportedly lagging behind competing video-generation models. **OpenAI's CEO of AGI deployment, Fidji Simo**, emphasized the need to "nail productivity... particularly on the business front," indicating a strategic shift away from projects like **Sora** and even deprioritizing "adult mode" sexting capabilities for **ChatGPT**. The decision highlights the intense competition in the AI video generation space, where market leaders like **Anthropic** and **Google** are formidable contenders.

> "We cannot miss this moment because we are distracted by side quests." 

Trevor Harries-Jones of the **Render Network Foundation** noted the lack of a strong competitive advantage for **Sora**, stating, "If your model is not the top at any one thing, it's very hard to get mass usership." This suggests that the saturated market and high operational costs ultimately led to **Sora's** demise, despite its short life leaving behind a legacy of questions about discerning real from AI-generated content.
