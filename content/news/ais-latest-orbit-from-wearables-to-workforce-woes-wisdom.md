## Meta is reportedly developing an AI pendant
![Meta is reportedly developing an AI pendant - TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/meta-ai-GettyImages-2264943773.jpg?w=1024)

**Meta** is reportedly venturing further into the realm of wearable AI with plans to test an **AI-powered pendant** within the next year. This development, revealed in a memo seen by *The Information*, signifies **Meta's** continued push into hardware that integrates artificial intelligence directly into users' daily lives.

The initiative appears to be a direct continuation of **Meta's** strategic acquisition of **Limitless**, an **AI device startup**, at the close of **2025**. **Limitless** was known for its own **AI pendant** designed to record conversations, a technology **Meta** stated would "accelerate our work to build AI-enabled wearables." The move comes despite earlier AI wearables struggling to gain traction, often due to privacy concerns, marketing missteps, or simply a lack of compelling utility for consumers.

> This device would presumably build on the work of Limitless, an AI device startup that Meta acquired at the end of 2025.

## Coders are refusing to work without AI — and that could come back to bite them
![Coders are refusing to work without AI — and that could come back to bite them - TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/coders-are-refusing-to-work-without-ai-and-that-could-come-back-to-bite-them.jpg?w=1024)

In **2026**, a striking trend has emerged in the developer community: coders are becoming increasingly unwilling to work on tasks without the assistance of **AI coding tools**. Research by the respected **AI research lab METR**, published in **February 2026**, highlighted this significant shift, noting that most developers now refuse to complete even limited tasks without AI.

While **AI** undoubtedly boosts coding speed and reported productivity, concerns are rising about the quality of the code produced. Earlier research in **2025** by **METR** showed that developers felt more productive with AI. However, there's a growing apprehension that this reliance might lead to a decline in code quality, potentially creating long-term issues for developers and the software industry at large. The ease and speed offered by AI could inadvertently mask a deeper understanding of coding principles, leading to unforeseen problems down the line.

> Most developers won’t work, even on a limited number of tasks, without AI anymore.

## So you’ve heard these AI terms and nodded along; let’s fix that
![So you’ve heard these AI terms and nodded along; let’s fix that - TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/GettyImages-ai-generated-eb728837-4a65-4ce4-b814-abd0c140d20c.jpg?w=1024)

The rapid evolution of artificial intelligence has not only transformed technology but also introduced a new lexicon that can often be intimidating. Terms like **LLMs** (Large Language Models), **RAG** (Retrieval Augmented Generation), and **RLHF** (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback) are now commonplace, yet frequently misunderstood. A new comprehensive glossary aims to bridge this knowledge gap, serving as a dynamic, living document to clarify these complex AI concepts.

One key term addressed is **AGI** (Artificial General Intelligence), a concept that, while still nebulous, generally refers to AI systems that can outperform humans across a wide range of tasks. **OpenAI CEO Sam Altman** has famously described **AGI** as comparable to "a median human that you could hire as a co-worker," while **OpenAI's charter** defines it as "highly autonomous systems that outperform humans at most economic tasks." This glossary serves as an essential resource for anyone looking to truly understand the language of AI rather than just nodding along.

> Artificial general intelligence, or AGI, is a nebulous term. But it generally refers to AI that’s more capable than the average human at many, if not most, tasks.

## What happens when companies become too AI-pilled?
![What happens when companies become too AI-pilled? - TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/2244026103.jpg?w=562)

The tech industry is currently grappling with a phenomenon dubbed "AI psychosis," where decision-makers, often far removed from the ground-level work, overzealously implement AI solutions, leading to significant job displacement. **Box founder Aaron Levie** highlighted this issue, pointing out that those least likely to understand a job's true nature are often the ones pushing for its AI replacement. This trend is evident in instances like **ClickUp's** recent **22% workforce reduction** in favor of **AI agents**.

This aggressive push for AI integration has not been without its critics or consequences. Tech layoffs in **2026** are already nearing the total of all **2025** layoffs, signaling a turbulent job market. Furthermore, users are reacting negatively to forced AI implementations, as seen with the **30% increase** in **DuckDuckGo installs** from users rejecting **Google's AI-heavy search results** in favor of traditional links. The situation raises crucial questions about striking a balance between AI's potential and its societal impact, as both AI proponents and skeptics find their views validated.

> The people deciding that AI can replace your job are also the ones least likely to understand what your job truly involves, according to Box founder Aaron Levie.

## Cognition’s Scott Wu says AI coding agents shouldn’t replace humans
![Cognition founder and CEO Scott Wu](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/Scott-wu-headshot-e1780070238342.png?w=1024)

**Scott Wu**, CEO of **Cognition**, recently made headlines after his two-year-old AI coding agent startup secured a colossal **$1 billion** funding round, pushing its valuation to **$26 billion**. **Cognition's** flagship product, **Devin**, is recognized as one of the pioneering and most successful AI coding agents, celebrated for its ability to "naturally own tasks end to end" and usher in a "world of self-driving software development."

Despite the advanced capabilities of **Devin** and the narrative surrounding AI's potential to automate jobs, **Wu** firmly states that **Cognition's** vision is not about replacing human developers. When asked if **Devin** could replace a mid-level **L4 programmer**, **Wu** responded, "We’ve never thought about it as replacing humans. I know it’s like a scenario, folks have said these things. It has never been our view." This perspective is particularly significant in a year like **2026**, marked by frequent tech layoffs and ongoing debates about AI's impact on employment, emphasizing a collaborative rather than substitutive role for AI in the workforce.

> "We’ve never thought about it as replacing humans. I know it’s like a scenario, folks have said these things. It has never been our view."
