## OpenAI, not yet public, raises $3B from retail investors in monster $122B fund raise - TechCrunch

![Sam Altman](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/GettyImages-2236544149.jpg?w=1024)

**OpenAI** has successfully closed its largest funding round to date, securing an astounding **$122 billion** at an **$852 billion** valuation. This significant capital infusion is earmarked to bolster **OpenAI's** resources for acquiring AI chips, expanding data center infrastructure, and attracting top talent, all critical expenditures as the company prepares for a potential public market debut this year.

The funding round saw prominent co-leaders including **SoftBank**, **Andreessen Horowitz**, **D.E. Shaw Ventures**, **MGX**, **TPG**, and **T. Rowe Price Associates**, with additional participation from tech giants **Amazon**, **Nvidia**, and **Microsoft**. Notably, approximately **$3 billion** of the total came from individual investors via bank channels, signaling a broader investor interest. Furthermore, **OpenAI** is set to be included in several **ARK Invest** ETFs, aiming to expand its shareholder base ahead of its anticipated IPO.

> **OpenAI's** colossal **$122 billion** funding round, including **$3 billion** from retail investors, underscores intense market confidence and a strategic move to broaden its ownership base before its projected IPO.

Beyond direct equity, **OpenAI** also expanded its revolving credit facility to approximately **$4.7 billion**, supported by leading global banks. This facility remains undrawn, suggesting a proactive effort to enhance financial flexibility for future compute and infrastructure investments rather than addressing immediate liquidity needs.

## OpenAI raises $122 billion to accelerate the next phase of AI - OpenAI

![OAIF Blogpost 1-1](https://images.ctfassets.net/kftzwdyauwt9/1369NNwJ3NhwM2y2U04sNA/9fcdfefd2e19135ac2e05b11fa985ce1/OAIF_Blogpost_1-1.png?w=3840&q=90&fm=webp)

**OpenAI** officially announced the closing of its latest funding round, raising **$122 billion** in committed capital at a post-money valuation of **$852 billion**. This massive investment is intended to "accelerate the next phase of AI," reinforcing **OpenAI's** position as a foundational infrastructure for AI development globally. The company highlights its role in enabling individuals and businesses to build innovative solutions, with **ChatGPT** serving as a potent distribution channel extending into the workplace.

The company emphasized the rapid growth and economic impact of its offerings. **OpenAI** claims to be the fastest technology platform to reach **10 million** and then **100 million** users, and is poised to become the fastest to reach **1 billion** weekly active users. Revenue growth has been equally impressive, with **$1 billion** generated within a year of **ChatGPT's** launch, escalating to **$1 billion** per quarter by the end of **2024**, and currently standing at **$2 billion** per month. This rate of revenue expansion is reportedly four times faster than companies that defined earlier internet and mobile eras, such as **Alphabet** and **Meta**.

> **OpenAI** is growing revenue four times faster than Internet and mobile giants, demonstrating unprecedented commercial and mission scale.

This funding round, anchored by strategic partners like **Amazon**, **NVIDIA**, and **SoftBank**, along with continued participation from **Microsoft**, reflects deep conviction from global capital in **OpenAI's** vision. The company believes that providing useful intelligence to people early and broadly is the fastest way to expand AI's benefits, driving productivity gains, accelerating scientific discovery, and expanding creative possibilities for organizations and individuals.

## Nomadic raises $8.4 million to wrangle the data pouring off autonomous vehicles - TechCrunch

![Mustafa Bal and Varun Krishnan, the co-founders of Nomadic AI.](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/founder_photo.jpeg?w=1024)

The proliferation of autonomous machines, from self-driving cars to robots, generates an enormous volume of video data for training and evaluation. However, much of this data, potentially **95%** of fleet data, often remains archived and underutilized because the current process of organizing and cataloging it relies heavily on time-consuming human review. **Nomadic AI**, a startup founded by CEO **Mustafa Bal** and CTO **Varun Krishnan**, aims to tackle this critical challenge.

**Nomadic AI's** solution involves a platform that transforms raw video footage into structured, searchable datasets using advanced vision language models. This innovation not only streamlines fleet monitoring but also enables the creation of unique datasets vital for reinforcement learning and faster iteration of AI models. A particular focus is on identifying "edge cases" – rare but highly valuable data points that are crucial for refining the intelligence of physical AI models.

> **Nomadic AI** is addressing a significant bottleneck in autonomous system development by using vision language models to transform vast amounts of video data into structured, searchable datasets, crucial for identifying rare edge cases.

The company announced an **$8.4 million** seed round, valuing the company at **$50 million**. The round was led by **TQ Ventures**, with contributions from **Pear VC** and **Jeff Dean**, among others. This funding will allow **Nomadic AI** to onboard more customers and further refine its platform. The startup also recently secured first prize at **Nvidia GTC's** pitch competition, highlighting the industry's recognition of its innovative approach.

## ScaleOps raises $130M to improve computing efficiency amid AI demand - TechCrunch

![artificial intelligence concept.](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/GettyImages-1136663877.jpg?w=1024)

Despite the booming demand for AI, companies are grappling with substantial waste in computing resources. GPUs often sit idle, workloads are frequently over-provisioned, and cloud costs continue their upward trajectory. **ScaleOps**, a startup that develops software to automatically manage and reallocate computing resources in real-time, believes this isn't a shortage of compute but rather a problem of mismanagement.

**ScaleOps** has successfully raised **$130 million** in a Series C funding round, bringing its valuation to **$800 million**. Led by **Insight Partners**, with participation from existing investors including **Lightspeed Venture Partners**, **NFX**, **Glilot Capital Partners**, and **Picture Capital**, this funding will enable the company to expand its efforts. **ScaleOps** claims its software can reduce cloud and AI infrastructure costs by as much as **80%**.

> **ScaleOps** secures **$130 million** to optimize AI and cloud infrastructure, addressing compute waste and potentially cutting costs by up to **80%** through real-time resource reallocation.

Co-founded in **2022** by CEO **Yodar Shafrir**, a former engineer at **Run:ai** (a GPU orchestration startup acquired by **Nvidia**), **ScaleOps** was born from firsthand experience with the difficulties companies face in managing complex AI workloads. While tools like **Kubernetes** are essential for running applications across large clusters, their reliance on static configurations struggles to adapt to dynamic demand, leading to inefficient GPU utilization and escalated costs. **ScaleOps** aims to provide a more dynamic solution to these production management challenges.

## Qodo raises $70M for code verification as AI coding scales - TechCrunch

![Abstract technology image of running program code on digital data wave.](https://techcrunch.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/GettyImages-2080252145.jpg?w=1024)

As AI coding tools rapidly generate billions of lines of code monthly, a new critical bottleneck has emerged: ensuring the reliability and intended functionality of this AI-generated software. **Qodo**, a startup specializing in AI agents for code review, testing, and governance, is strategically positioning itself to address this challenge, betting that robust verification will define the next phase of software development.

**Qodo**, headquartered in New York, has successfully closed a **$70 million** Series B funding round, bringing its total funding to **$120 million**. The round was led by **Qumra Capital**, with participation from a diverse group of investors including **Maor Ventures**, **Phoenix Venture Partners**, **S Ventures**, **Square Peg**, **Susa Ventures**, **TLV Partners**, **Vine Ventures**, and notable individuals like **Peter Welender** (**OpenAI**) and **Clara Shih** (**Meta**).

> **Qodo's** **$70 million** Series B underscores the rising importance of code verification and governance for AI-generated code, aiming to improve trust and reliability in enterprise AI adoption.

The company's mission is to establish a crucial layer that enhances trust in AI-generated code, particularly as enterprises increasingly adopt tools such as **OpenClaw** and **Claude Code**. Many organizations are discovering that increased code output speed does not inherently translate into reliable or secure software. Unlike most AI review tools that focus on isolated code changes, **Qodo's** platform assesses how code modifications impact entire systems, integrating organizational standards, historical context, and risk tolerance to enable more confident management of AI-generated code. The company was founded in **2022** by **Itamar Friedman**, who previously co-founded **Visualead** and led machine vision at **Alibaba**.
